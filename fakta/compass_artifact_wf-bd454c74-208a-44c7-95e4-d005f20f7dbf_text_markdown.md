# The assassination of President John F. Kennedy: a comprehensive reference document

**On November 22, 1963, at 12:30 PM Central Standard Time, President John Fitzgerald Kennedy was fatally shot while riding in a motorcade through Dealey Plaza in Dallas, Texas.** This event remains the most investigated, debated, and mythologized crime in American history. What follows is a maximum-detail factual reference covering every dimension of the assassination — timeline, locations, people, evidence, investigations, theories, and context — assembled as foundational material for game development.

---

## 1. Minute-by-minute timeline of November 22, 1963

### Morning in Fort Worth

The Kennedys spent the night of November 21 in **Suite 850 of the Hotel Texas** in Fort Worth after arriving at Carswell Air Force Base at 11:07 PM the previous evening. At approximately **7:10 AM**, JFK dressed, lacing a surgical corset for his chronic back problems and wrapping an elastic bandage in a figure-eight over his hips. This rigid back brace would have a tragic consequence: it held him upright after the first bullet hit, making him a stationary target for the fatal shot.

At roughly **8:30 AM**, JFK looked out his hotel window at the platform where he would address a crowd and made a chilling observation: **"If someone wanted to get you, it wouldn't be very difficult... All you'd have to do is get up in a high building with a high-powered rifle with a telescopic sight, and there's nothing anybody can do."** He delivered an outdoor speech at approximately 8:45 AM, joking about Jackie's absence: **"Mrs. Kennedy is organizing herself. It takes longer, but, of course, she looks better than we do when she does it."**

At roughly **10:00 AM**, Assistant Press Secretary Malcolm Kilduff showed the Kennedys a hostile full-page ad in the *Dallas Morning News* — bordered in black like a funeral notice, accusing JFK of being soft on communism. Kennedy told Jackie: **"We're heading into nut country today."** The presidential party departed Hotel Texas at **10:40 AM** for Carswell Air Force Base. Air Force One departed at approximately **11:20 AM** for the 13-minute flight to Dallas.

### Arrival at Love Field, Dallas

Air Force Two carrying Vice President Johnson landed at **11:35 AM**. Air Force One touched down at **11:38–11:40 AM** to a large, enthusiastic crowd. Jackie Kennedy received a bouquet of **red roses**. The Kennedys worked the fence line for approximately 10 minutes, shaking hands with supporters.

The **presidential limousine** (SS-100-X), a modified 1961 Lincoln Continental convertible, was configured with six occupants: **Secret Service Agent William Greer** (driver), **SAIC Roy Kellerman** (front passenger), **Governor John Connally** (right jump seat), **Nellie Connally** (left jump seat), **President Kennedy** (right rear), and **Jacqueline Kennedy** (left rear). The transparent bubble top — not bulletproof — was removed because the sun was shining, per JFK's preference.

### The motorcade through Dallas

The motorcade departed Love Field at approximately **11:55 AM** for its **9.5-mile route** to the Dallas Trade Mart luncheon. The route: Mockingbird Lane → Lemmon Avenue → Turtle Creek Boulevard → Cedar Springs Road → Harwood Street → **Main Street** (the traditional Dallas parade route) → right turn onto **Houston Street** → sharp left turn onto **Elm Street** → through Dealey Plaza → under the Triple Underpass → Stemmons Freeway → Trade Mart.

Crowds were dense — an estimated **150,000–200,000 people** — the largest turnout Dallas had seen for a visiting president. People hung from windows, pressing eight to ten deep along Main Street. The motorcade slowed to **10–15 mph** in the densest areas. JFK twice ordered the car stopped to greet children.

At approximately **12:29 PM**, as the motorcade entered Dealey Plaza, Nellie Connally turned and said: **"Mr. President, you can't say Dallas doesn't love you."** Kennedy replied: **"No, they sure can't."** These are believed to be among his last words.

### The shots at Dealey Plaza — 12:30 PM CST

The motorcade made a sharp **120-degree left turn from Houston Street onto Elm Street**, slowing to approximately **10–11 mph** on the downhill slope. The Hertz clock atop the Texas School Book Depository changed from 12:29 to **12:30** as the limousine entered the kill zone.

**Abraham Zapruder**, standing on a concrete pedestal near the grassy knoll pergola, filmed the entire sequence with his Bell & Howell 8mm camera at **18.3 frames per second** — 486 frames over 26.6 seconds.

**First shot (missed):** Estimated at approximately **Zapruder frames Z150–Z160**. The Warren Commission concluded this shot missed entirely, possibly deflected by a tree branch. The bullet may have fragmented and struck a curb near bystander James Tague at the Triple Underpass.

**Second shot (the "single bullet"):** Impact at approximately **Z210–Z225**. JFK disappears behind the Stemmons Freeway sign around Z207 and emerges at Z225 with hands rising to his throat. At **Z224**, Governor Connally's lapel visibly flips — evidence of bullet impact. This bullet entered JFK's upper back, exited his throat, struck Connally in the back, exited his chest, went through his right wrist, and lodged in his left thigh.

**Third shot (fatal):** Impact at **Zapruder frame Z313** — the catastrophic head shot. Kennedy's head snaps **forward 2.3 inches** from Z312 to Z313 (consistent with a rear-entry impact), then lurches violently **"back and to the left"** in subsequent frames. The right side of Kennedy's skull explodes in a cloud of brain matter, blood, and bone. Jackie Kennedy cried: **"They've killed my husband! I have his brains in my hand!"**

The Warren Commission estimated all three shots were fired within **4.8 to 7.9 seconds**. Most witnesses reported hearing **three shots**.

### Immediate aftermath

**Clint Hill**, the Secret Service agent assigned to Jackie's detail, leaped from the follow-up car and sprinted toward the limousine. He reached the car at approximately **Z371**, climbing onto the trunk as Jackie — in apparent shock — crawled across it reaching for a piece of her husband's skull. Hill pushed her back into the seat and shielded both Kennedys with his body. He later said that if he'd reacted **"five-tenths of a second faster,"** the president would be alive.

Agent Kellerman radioed: **"To the nearest hospital, quick!"** The limousine accelerated to approximately **80 mph** along Stemmons Freeway. UPI reporter **Merriman Smith**, in the press pool car, filed the first report at **12:34 PM**: **"Three shots were fired today at the president's motorcade in downtown Dallas."** Smith would win the 1964 Pulitzer Prize.

### Parkland Memorial Hospital (5201 Harry Hines Boulevard)

The limousine arrived at Parkland at approximately **12:36–12:38 PM**, four miles from Dealey Plaza. Kennedy was wheeled into **Trauma Room 1**. **Dr. Charles Carrico**, the first physician, found slow agonal breathing, a heartbeat but no pulse or blood pressure. He noted two wounds: one in the lower anterior neck and a massive wound in the occipital region of the skull.

**Dr. Malcolm Perry** performed a tracheotomy, cutting directly through the bullet wound in the throat. **Dr. Kemp Clark**, chief of neurosurgery, arrived and began closed-chest cardiac massage. Approximately **15 doctors** worked on Kennedy, administering Ringer's solution, type O-negative blood, and **300 mg of hydrocortisone** (Carrico knew Kennedy had Addison's disease). No electrical cardiac activity was ever detected.

At **1:00 PM CST**, Dr. Kemp Clark officially pronounced President Kennedy dead. **Father Oscar Huber** of Holy Trinity Catholic Church administered last rites. Jackie Kennedy placed her wedding ring on her husband's finger and kissed his bare foot before leaving.

LBJ, held in a windowless room at Parkland, heard Kilduff address him as **"Mr. President"** for the first time. He ordered the death announcement delayed until he'd left the hospital, saying: **"We don't know whether this is a worldwide conspiracy, whether they are after me as well."** LBJ departed Parkland at **1:26 PM** and arrived at Love Field at **1:33 PM**.

### LBJ sworn in

At **2:38 PM CST**, Lyndon Baines Johnson took the oath of office aboard Air Force One at Love Field. **U.S. District Judge Sarah T. Hughes** — the first woman to administer the presidential oath — officiated. Jackie Kennedy stood at his left, still wearing her blood-stained pink Chanel suit. The ceremony lasted **28 seconds**. Johnson placed his left hand on a Catholic missal (often misidentified as a Bible). Air Force One departed for Washington at **2:47 PM**.

### Oswald's movements

**7:23 AM:** Lee Harvey Oswald arrived at the Texas School Book Depository with co-worker **Buell Wesley Frazier**, carrying a long paper package. When asked, Oswald said: **"Oh, just some curtain rods."** The Warren Commission concluded it contained his disassembled Mannlicher-Carcano rifle.

**~11:55 AM:** Oswald was last seen on the sixth floor by co-worker Charles Givens, approximately 35 minutes before the shooting.

**~12:31–12:32 PM (90 seconds after shots):** Dallas motorcycle officer **Marrion Baker** raced into the TSBD and, with superintendent **Roy Truly**, encountered Oswald in the **second-floor lunchroom**. Baker pointed his pistol at Oswald. Truly identified him as an employee. Oswald appeared calm, possibly holding a Coke. Baker let him pass.

**~12:33 PM:** Oswald left the TSBD through the front door. He walked seven blocks east and boarded a Marsalis city bus. Traffic congestion caused by the assassination forced him to exit after a few blocks (the bus transfer was later found on his person). He hired a taxi driven by **William Whaley** and was dropped near his rooming house.

**~1:00 PM:** Oswald arrived at **1026 North Beckley Avenue** in Oak Cliff. Housekeeper **Earlene Roberts** noted he seemed "in quite a hurry." He went to his room, retrieved a **.38 Smith & Wesson revolver** and a jacket, and departed within 3–4 minutes. Roberts noticed a Dallas police car drive slowly past and honk.

**~1:15 PM:** Officer **J.D. Tippit**, patrolling in Oak Cliff, pulled alongside Oswald near **10th Street and Patton Avenue** (0.9 miles from the rooming house). After exchanging words through the car window, Tippit exited his vehicle. Oswald shot him **four times** with the .38 revolver. Thirteen witnesses saw Oswald shoot Tippit or flee. Five identified him in police lineups that evening.

**~1:40 PM:** After fleeing on foot and discarding his jacket, Oswald ducked into the **Texas Theatre** (231 W. Jefferson Boulevard) without paying. Shoe store manager **Johnny Brewer** noticed the suspicious behavior and alerted ticket seller **Julia Postal**, who called police.

**~1:50 PM:** Police entered the darkened theater. Officer **M.N. "Nick" McDonald** approached Oswald, who stood and said: **"Well, it's all over now."** Oswald punched McDonald and pulled his revolver. The gun misfired or was grabbed. After a struggle, Oswald was subdued. He was charged with Tippit's murder at **7:10 PM** and with JFK's murder at **1:35 AM on November 23**.

### The Bethesda autopsy

Air Force One arrived at Andrews Air Force Base at **5:58 PM EST**. The body was taken to **Bethesda Naval Hospital** (Jackie chose Bethesda because JFK was a Navy veteran). The autopsy began at approximately **8:00 PM EST**, conducted by **Commander James Humes** and **Commander J. Thornton Boswell**, assisted by **Lt. Colonel Pierre Finck** — notably, none were board-certified forensic pathologists. Admiral George Burkley urged: **"All we need is the bullet."** The autopsy concluded in the early morning hours of November 23.

### November 24 — Ruby shoots Oswald

Oswald endured approximately **12 hours of interrogation** by Captain **J.W. Fritz** — with no recordings or transcripts made. He repeatedly denied involvement: **"I didn't shoot anybody"** and **"I'm just a patsy."**

At **11:17 AM** on November 24, nightclub owner **Jack Ruby** walked to the Western Union office at 1044 Main Street to wire **$25** to stripper **Karen "Little Lynn" Carlin**. He then walked to Dallas Police HQ and entered the basement, likely via the Main Street ramp.

At **11:21 AM CST**, as Oswald was escorted by detective **Jim Leavelle** (handcuffed to Oswald's right wrist), Ruby lunged from the crowd and fired a single shot from a **.38 Colt Cobra revolver** into Oswald's lower left abdomen. Ruby shouted: **"You killed my president, you rat!"** The shooting was broadcast **live on national television** — the first murder ever committed on live TV. Oswald died at Parkland Hospital at **1:07 PM**.

## 2. Key locations and spatial relationships

### Dealey Plaza

**Location:** West End Historic District, downtown Dallas, at the convergence of Elm, Main, and Commerce Streets. **Size: 3.07 acres**, triangular, with Houston Street forming the eastern base and the Triple Underpass at the western apex. Designated a **National Historic Landmark** on November 22, 1993.

Three streets trisect the plaza running east-to-west: **Elm Street** (northernmost, the assassination route), **Main Street** (center), and **Commerce Street** (southernmost), separated by grassy medians. Two Art Deco concrete pergolas sit symmetrically on the north and south slopes — Zapruder filmed from the **north pergola**. Two rectangular reflecting pools extend along Houston Street.

### Texas School Book Depository (411 Elm Street)

A seven-story **red-brick Romanesque Revival warehouse** built in 1901, leased in 1963 to a textbook distribution company. The **"sniper's nest"** was in the **southeast corner of the sixth floor** — the second window from the east end on the south wall. Cardboard book boxes were arranged as a barricade and gun rest, shielding the shooter from view. Three spent 6.5mm cartridge cases were found near the open window; the rifle was found nearby, hidden between boxes.

**Shot distances from the 6th floor window:**
- First shot (missed): ~**175 feet**
- Second shot (single bullet): ~**190 feet**
- Fatal head shot: **265.3 feet** (81 meters)
- Height above Elm Street: ~**60 feet**
- The window required only ~**7.5 degrees of tracking** for the fatal shot, as the target was moving nearly directly away

The building now houses the **Sixth Floor Museum** (opened 1989), with the sniper's nest reconstructed behind Plexiglass.

### The grassy knoll

A small sloping hill on the **northwest side of Dealey Plaza**, rising from Elm Street's north sidewalk toward a parking lot and railroad yard. Key features in 1963: the north pergola, a **5-foot-high wooden stockade fence** (~169 feet long) at the top, and thick live oak trees. The fence was approximately **40–50 feet** from the motorcade route at its nearest point. **35 of 104 Dealey Plaza earwitnesses** reported hearing shots from the direction of the grassy knoll.

### Triple Underpass

A concrete railroad overpass at the western apex of Dealey Plaza where Elm, Main, and Commerce Streets converge. Built by the WPA in **1936**. Distance from the east base of the plaza: ~**500 feet**. The fatal head shot occurred approximately **200–250 feet** east of the underpass. Several railroad workers, including **S.M. Holland**, stood on the overpass and witnessed the shooting.

### Other key locations

| Location | Address | Distance from Dealey Plaza |
|---|---|---|
| Parkland Memorial Hospital | 5201 Harry Hines Blvd | ~4 miles (4–5 min at emergency speed) |
| Oswald's rooming house | 1026 N. Beckley Ave, Oak Cliff | ~3–4 miles |
| Tippit murder scene | 10th St & Patton Ave, Oak Cliff | ~4 miles |
| Texas Theatre | 231 W. Jefferson Blvd, Oak Cliff | ~4.5 miles |
| Jack Ruby's Carousel Club | 1312½ Commerce St | ~5 blocks |
| Dallas Police HQ | 106 S. Harwood St | ~3–4 blocks |
| Love Field Airport | 8008 Herb Kelleher Way | ~10 miles (motorcade route) |

From the rooming house to the Tippit scene: **~0.9 miles** (13–16 minute walk). From the Tippit scene to the Texas Theatre: **~0.6 miles** (6–8 minute walk/run).

## 3. Key people and witnesses

### The motorcade principals

**Jacqueline Kennedy** sat in the left rear seat wearing her iconic **pink Chanel-style suit** and matching pillbox hat. After the fatal head shot, she climbed onto the trunk of the limousine — she later had no memory of doing so. Agent Hill believed she was reaching for a piece of skull. At Parkland, she exchanged rings with her dying husband, then kissed his bare foot before leaving.

**Governor John Connally** was hit by the "single bullet" and suffered wounds to his back, chest, right wrist, and left thigh. He survived but never accepted the single-bullet theory, insisting he was hit by a separate shot. **Nellie Connally** pulled her wounded husband into her lap and shielded him.

**Driver William Greer** controversially slowed the vehicle after the first shots rather than accelerating. Senator Ralph Yarborough, riding two cars back, stated the motorcade **"slowed to what seemed to me a complete stop."** At Parkland, Greer tearfully told Jackie: **"Oh, Mrs. Kennedy, oh my God... I didn't mean to do it. I didn't hear, I should have swerved the car."**

### Dealey Plaza witnesses

**Abraham Zapruder** (1905–1970) stood on a concrete pedestal near the north pergola, steadied by his secretary Marilyn Sitzman. He captured the assassination on his Bell & Howell Zoomatic 8mm camera. After seeing the head shot through his viewfinder, he screamed: **"They killed him, they killed him."** He sold the film to *Life* magazine for **$150,000** (totaling ~$1.58 million today). The U.S. government purchased it from the Zapruder estate in 1999 for **$16 million**.

**Howard Brennan** sat on a concrete retaining wall at the southwest corner of Elm and Houston, ~120 feet from the sixth-floor window. He watched a man at the window "standing up and resting against the left window sill, with gun shouldered to his right shoulder... taking positive aim and fired his last shot." He described the gunman as a slender white male, approximately **5'10", 165–175 pounds**. At the police lineup, Brennan said Oswald **"looks like him"** but wouldn't make a positive ID, fearing for his family. He later made a firm identification.

**Jean Hill**, "the Lady in Red," stood on the south side of Elm approximately **21 feet** from Kennedy at the moment of the fatal shot. She claimed to see a puff of smoke from the grassy knoll and a man running, whom she later believed was Jack Ruby. She reported hearing **four to six shots**.

**Mary Moorman** snapped her famous Polaroid between Zapruder frames 315–316 — less than **one-sixth of a second** after the fatal head shot — capturing the limousine with the grassy knoll in the background. Conspiracy researchers later claimed to see a **"badge man"** figure behind the stockade fence, though the Rochester Institute of Technology found the area too underexposed to identify any figures.

**Louie Steven Witt**, the **"Umbrella Man,"** stood on the north side of Elm near the Stemmons Freeway sign, pumping an open black umbrella on a clear sunny day. In 1978 HSCA testimony, he explained it was a protest referencing **Joseph P. Kennedy Sr.'s appeasement** of Hitler — the umbrella symbolized Neville Chamberlain. He famously said: **"If The Guinness Book of World Records had a category for people who were at the wrong place at the wrong time, doing the wrong thing, I would be No. 1."**

**James Tague** stood near the Triple Underpass and was cut on the right cheek by a concrete chip dislodged by a bullet fragment. His wounding forced the Warren Commission to account for a missed shot, which in turn **necessitated the single-bullet theory** — since three cartridge cases were found, one had to have missed, one was the fatal head shot, and one had to explain all other wounds.

**S.M. Holland**, a railroad supervisor on the overpass, provided some of the most detailed testimony about grassy knoll activity: **"I heard the report and saw the smoke come out about 6 or 8 feet above the ground, right out from under those trees."** He ran behind the fence after the shooting and found footprints in the mud and cigarette butts.

**Lee Bowers** operated a railroad switching tower ~120 yards north of the knoll with an unobstructed view of the stockade fence. He observed two men near the fence before the shooting and, at the moment of the shots, **"a flash of light or smoke or something which caused me to feel like something out of the ordinary had occurred."** Bowers died on August 9, 1966, in a single-car accident under circumstances many consider suspicious.

### Parkland doctors vs. Bethesda autopsy team

**Dr. Malcolm Perry** performed the tracheotomy and, at the press conference, initially described the throat wound as an **"entrance wound"** — contradicting the lone-gunman-from-behind theory. Dr. Robert McClelland later recalled that someone who may have been a Secret Service agent told Perry: **"You must never, ever say that was an entrance wound again if you know what's good for you."** Perry subsequently told the Warren Commission the wound could have been an exit wound.

**Dr. Robert McClelland** stood at the head of the gurney and described a **large wound in the back of the President's head** — contradicting the Bethesda autopsy photographs, which show damage primarily on the right side. Over 20 Parkland witnesses described the large wound in the rear of the skull, while the Bethesda autopsy placed it on the right side and front.

At Bethesda, **Dr. James Humes** led the autopsy but was not a forensic pathologist. He admitted under oath to the ARRB in the 1990s that he **burned both his original autopsy notes and the first draft of the autopsy report** in his home fireplace. **Dr. Pierre Finck** disclosed at the 1969 Clay Shaw trial that when Humes asked **"Who is in charge here?"**, an unidentified **Army General stepped forward** — suggesting military brass were overruling the doctors.

## 4. Physical evidence

### The rifle

The **Mannlicher-Carcano Model 91/38** (serial number **C2766**), a 6.5x52mm Italian bolt-action infantry carbine, was manufactured in 1940 at the Royal Arms Factory in Terni, Italy. Overall length: **40.2 inches**. It was purchased via mail order from **Klein's Sporting Goods in Chicago** on March 12, 1963, under the alias **"A. Hidell"** for **$19.95** plus postage. The rifle was equipped with an inexpensive Japanese-made **4x18 Ordnance Optics** telescopic sight, which was found to be improperly mounted and shimmed.

Minimum bolt-action cycling time: approximately **2.3 seconds** between shots (the HSCA found it possible to fire two shots within **1.66 seconds** using open sights). Four firearms experts positively identified all recovered bullets and fragments as fired from this specific weapon.

**Oswald's palm print** was found on the underside of the barrel (visible only with the wooden foregrip removed). Controversy persists: **Lt. J.C. Day** lifted the print but did not photograph it first; the **FBI's Sebastian Latona** found no palm print traces when he examined the rifle; the print was not forwarded to the FBI until **November 26** — two days after Oswald was murdered. FBI agents visited Oswald's body at the morgue on November 24 with a crime kit, fueling speculation the print could have been taken posthumously.

### The "magic bullet" (CE 399)

Found on a stretcher at Parkland Hospital by maintenance engineer **Darrell Tomlinson**, CE 399 is a 6.5mm Carcano copper-jacketed bullet, nearly intact — slightly flattened at the base with minor lead extrusion. Weight: **158.6 grains** (normal ~161 grains, meaning only **2–3 grains lost**). It was positively matched to the C2766 rifle.

**Chain of custody problems:** Tomlinson couldn't identify which stretcher the bullet was on. Parkland security chief **O.P. Wright** told researcher Josiah Thompson the bullet he handled was *pointed-nosed*, whereas CE 399 is round-nosed. In **2023**, retired Secret Service Agent **Paul Landis** told the *New York Times* that he had retrieved a bullet from the rear seat of the limousine and placed it on *Kennedy's* stretcher — not Connally's — potentially upending the established narrative.

### The single-bullet theory trajectory

Developed by junior Warren Commission counsel **Arlen Specter**, the theory holds that CE 399 entered JFK's upper back, transited his neck without hitting bone, exited his throat, entered Connally's right back below the armpit, shattered his **fifth rib** exiting below the right nipple, entered the back of his right wrist shattering the **radius bone**, exited the palm side, and embedded a fragment in his left thigh — **seven wounds through two men**.

The theory works geometrically because Connally's jump seat was positioned **3 inches lower** and **6 inches inboard** (to the left) of Kennedy's rear seat. When seat positions and body positions are properly modeled, the trajectory forms a **nearly straight line** from the sixth-floor window. **Dale Myers' Emmy-winning 3D computer animation** (2003) and **NASA scientist Thomas Canning's** trajectory study for the HSCA both confirmed the alignment.

### Zapruder film key frames

| Frame | Event |
|---|---|
| Z133 | JFK visible waving to crowd |
| Z150–166 | Possible first shot (missed) |
| Z207–215 | JFK disappears behind Stemmons Freeway sign |
| Z224 | Connally's lapel visibly flips (bullet impact) |
| Z225–230 | JFK emerges, hands at throat, clearly hit |
| Z312 | Frame immediately before fatal head shot |
| **Z313** | **Fatal head shot — catastrophic skull damage, forward snap of 2.3 inches** |
| Z314–316 | "Back and to the left" motion; brain matter ejection visible |
| Z340+ | Jackie climbing onto trunk; Hill approaching |

The **forward snap** (Z312→313) is consistent with a rear-entry bullet. The subsequent backward motion is attributed to a **neuromuscular "decerebrate response"** and a **jet effect** from material exiting the right-front wound. Physicist Nicholas Nalli's 2018 study in *Heliyon* concluded the Zapruder film shows Kennedy **"being shot from behind and not from the infamous grassy knoll."**

### Autopsy controversies

The most significant forensic dispute concerns the **head wound description**: Parkland doctors (over 20 witnesses) consistently described a large avulsive wound in the **right rear** of the skull, while the Bethesda autopsy placed the major wound on the **right side/front**. Autopsy photographs in the National Archives show the back of the head largely intact, contradicting both Parkland witnesses and some Bethesda witnesses (including FBI Agent O'Neill, who stated **"there was a massive wound in the right rear of the head"**).

Kennedy's **brain** was removed during autopsy, preserved in formaldehyde, and placed in a stainless steel container at the National Archives. By 1966, the brain, tissue slides, and blood samples had **vanished**. ARRB chief analyst Douglas Horne concluded the brain photographs in the Archives were **"not of President Kennedy's brain"** based on testimony from autopsy witnesses and photographer John Stringer. The supplemental autopsy report listed brain weight at **1,500 grams** — approximately normal for an undamaged brain, despite witnesses describing massive brain loss.

The **paraffin test** on Oswald's hands tested **positive** for nitrates, but his right cheek tested **negative** — seemingly inconsistent with having fired a rifle. However, FBI experiments showed the test was unreliable in both directions: an agent who fired the same rifle tested negative on both hands and cheek.

## 5. The Warren Commission

Established **November 29, 1963** by Executive Order 11130, the President's Commission was chaired by **Chief Justice Earl Warren** and included Senators Richard Russell and John Sherman Cooper, Representatives Hale Boggs and Gerald Ford, **former CIA Director Allen Dulles** (fired by JFK after Bay of Pigs — a glaring conflict of interest), and John J. McCloy. General Counsel was **J. Lee Rankin**; junior counsel **Arlen Specter** developed the single-bullet theory.

The Commission examined **552 witnesses**, compiled **3,154 exhibits**, and released its **888-page report** plus 26 volumes on September 24, 1964. Core conclusions: three shots from the sixth floor of the TSBD; Oswald acted alone; Ruby acted alone; no conspiracy.

**Criticisms were substantial and immediate.** The CIA withheld information about plots to assassinate Castro. FBI Director Hoover appeared determined to close the case within 24 hours. No transcript existed of Oswald's 12+ hours of interrogation. Three of seven commissioners — **Russell, Boggs, and Cooper** — considered the single-bullet theory improbable. Russell specifically requested a dissenting footnote; Warren refused. A 2014 CIA internal report revealed that LBJ, RFK, and **four of seven commissioners** expressed private skepticism. By 1976, **81% of Americans** disputed the Warren Report. A 2023 Gallup poll showed **65%** still believe in a broader conspiracy.

## 6. The HSCA and acoustic evidence

The House Select Committee on Assassinations was formed on **September 15, 1976**, in the wake of Watergate and the Church Committee's revelations about CIA-Mafia assassination plots. Chief Counsel **G. Robert Blakey** (author of the RICO Act) led the investigation.

The HSCA's bombshell finding rested on a **Dictabelt recording** from a Dallas police motorcycle whose radio microphone was stuck open. Acoustics experts **Bolt, Beranek and Newman** identified impulse patterns consistent with gunshots, and **Weiss and Aschkenasy** of Queens College refined the analysis, concluding with **95%+ probability** that **four shots** were fired — one from the grassy knoll.

The HSCA concluded in 1979: **"President John F. Kennedy was probably assassinated as a result of a conspiracy."** They could not identify the second gunman but believed organized crime was likely involved. They upheld the single-bullet theory and cleared the CIA, FBI, and Secret Service as organizations while noting individual members could not be excluded.

**The debunking:** Amateur researcher **Steve Barber** discovered "cross-talk" on the recording — a spoken phrase from another police channel that could be independently timed, placing the alleged gunshot sounds **approximately one minute after** the assassination. The **National Academy of Sciences** panel (1982), chaired by Harvard's Norman Ramsey, unanimously concluded: **"The acoustic analyses do not demonstrate that there was a grassy knoll shot."** The DOJ formally concluded in 1988 that **"no persuasive evidence can be identified to support the theory of a conspiracy."** The acoustic evidence remains contested among some researchers but is widely considered discredited by mainstream science.

A critical post-investigation revelation: the CIA's designated liaison to the HSCA, **George Joannides**, was actually the chief of the CIA's Psychological Warfare branch in Miami in November 1963 — making him a **material witness** rather than an impartial liaison. Blakey called this **"little short of outrageous"** and stated the CIA **"set me up."**

## 7. Lee Harvey Oswald

Born **October 18, 1939**, in New Orleans. His father died two months before his birth. His mother Marguerite was described by social workers as "rigid" and "self-involved." Lee was placed in an orphan asylum at age three and attended **12 different schools** by age 17. At 13, he was sent to Youth House in Manhattan where psychiatrist **Dr. Renatus Hartogs** diagnosed him with **"personality pattern disturbance with schizoid features and passive-aggressive tendencies"** while noting his "superior mental resources." When asked if he preferred boys or girls, Lee replied: **"I dislike everybody."**

Oswald enlisted in the **Marines** at 17, trained as a radar operator, and was stationed at **Atsugi Air Base in Japan** — a launch point for CIA U-2 spy plane missions. He began studying Russian (nicknamed **"Oswaldskovich"**) and scored as a **"sharpshooter"** in marksmanship (middle tier). He was court-martialed twice.

In October 1959, Oswald defected to the Soviet Union. When the Soviets initially refused him, he **attempted suicide** by slitting his wrist, writing: *"How easy to Die and A Sweet Death, (to violins)."* He told the American Embassy he intended to give the Soviets radar secrets. He was sent to **Minsk**, given an apartment and factory job at the Horizon radio plant. He married **Marina Prusakova** on April 30, 1961 — her uncle **Ilya Prusakov** was a colonel in the MVD (Ministry of Internal Affairs).

Oswald returned to the US in June 1962 with State Department assistance. In Dallas, he was befriended by **George de Mohrenschildt**, a Russian émigré petroleum geologist with documented CIA connections. De Mohrenschildt later claimed the CIA's Dallas contact, **J. Walton Moore**, gave him Oswald's address and suggested he meet him: **"I would never have contacted Oswald in a million years if Moore had not sanctioned it."** De Mohrenschildt died by shotgun wound on **March 29, 1977** — the same day an HSCA investigator arrived to question him.

On **April 10, 1963**, Oswald fired at right-wing **General Edwin Walker** through his home window with the same Mannlicher-Carcano rifle. The bullet narrowly missed. Marina confirmed this in testimony.

During the summer of 1963 in New Orleans, Oswald created a one-man **Fair Play for Cuba Committee** chapter while simultaneously approaching anti-Castro activists — a paradox deepened by the fact that his FPCC leaflets bore the address **544 Camp Street**, the same building as anti-Castro operative **Guy Banister's** detective agency. The connection to Banister's associate **David Ferrie** — who had been Oswald's Civil Air Patrol captain as a teenager — added another layer of mystery.

In late September 1963, Oswald traveled to **Mexico City**, visiting the Cuban and Soviet embassies seeking visas. He met with KGB officer **Valeriy Kostikov** of Department 13 (assassinations). CIA surveillance captured someone **impersonating Oswald** in phone calls to the Soviet Embassy; the "mystery man" photo from CIA cameras was clearly not Oswald. FBI Director Hoover told LBJ the next day: **"That picture and the tape do not correspond to this man's voice, nor to his appearance."**

During 12+ hours of interrogation after his arrest — with no recordings made — Oswald declared: **"I'm just a patsy."** He requested lawyer **John Abt** (a Communist Party-affiliated attorney) but was never provided effective legal counsel. He never confessed.

## 8. Jack Ruby

Born **Jacob Leon Rubenstein** around March 25, 1911, in Chicago's Maxwell Street area to Polish-Jewish immigrant parents. A troubled childhood marked by foster homes and juvenile delinquency. As a youth, he ran errands for **Al Capone's organization** and was friendly with **Lenny Patrick** and **Dave Yaras**, later gunmen for Sam Giancana's Chicago Outfit. Nicknamed **"Sparky"** for his volatile temper.

Ruby moved to Dallas in **1947**, changed his name, and operated the **Carousel Club** (strip club) at 1312½ Commerce Street and the **Vegas Club**. He cultivated extraordinary access to Dallas police by providing **free liquor, food, and favors**, making him a constant, familiar presence at police headquarters.

**Organized crime connections were significant.** His phone records in the weeks before the assassination showed calls to: **Irwin Weiner** (Chicago mob bondsman), **Nofio Pecora** (Carlos Marcello lieutenant), **Barney Baker** (Jimmy Hoffa aide), **Lewis McWillie** (Havana casino manager connected to Santos Trafficante), and **Dave Yaras** (Chicago syndicate hit man). The HSCA strongly disagreed with the Warren Commission's claim of "no significant link" between Ruby and organized crime.

On November 22, Ruby was at the *Dallas Morning News* offices when he learned of the assassination. White House correspondent **Seth Kantor** testified he encountered Ruby at Parkland Hospital around 1:30 PM — the Warren Commission said Kantor was mistaken, but the HSCA determined **"he probably was not."** That night, Ruby appeared at the midnight press conference at police HQ and **corrected DA Henry Wade** when Wade misidentified Oswald's organization, interjecting: **"Fair Play for Cuba Committee."**

On November 24, Ruby received a call from stripper **Karen Carlin** asking for money, wired her $25 at Western Union (receipt timestamped **11:17 AM**), walked one block to police headquarters, and shot Oswald **four minutes later** at 11:21 AM. He left his beloved dog **Sheba** in the car — often cited as evidence against premeditation.

At his Warren Commission deposition, Ruby made extraordinary statements: **"I want to tell the truth, and I can't tell it here... my life is in danger."** He begged to be taken to Washington. He said: **"Everything pertaining to what's happening has never come to the surface. The world will never know the true facts... The people who had so much to gain... will never let the true facts come above board to the world."** Warren declined to bring him to Washington.

Ruby was convicted of murder on March 14, 1964, and sentenced to death. His conviction was overturned in October 1966. Before a new trial could begin, he was diagnosed with lung cancer and died at **Parkland Hospital** on **January 3, 1967** — the same hospital where both Kennedy and Oswald had died.

## 9. Conspiracy theories

### The second shooter on the grassy knoll

The most persistent theory holds that at least one additional gunman fired from the stockade fence atop the grassy knoll. Evidence cited: **52% of earwitnesses** pointed to the knoll area; Holland's smoke sighting; Bowers' "flash of light"; the Newman family hitting the ground believing shots came from behind them; and the "back and to the left" head movement at Z313. The HSCA's acoustic analysis (later challenged) suggested a fourth shot from the knoll. Modern physics explains the head snap through jet effect and neuromuscular response, and all recovered bullet fragments matched Oswald's rifle.

### CIA involvement

JFK fired CIA Director **Allen Dulles** after Bay of Pigs and reportedly vowed to **"splinter the CIA into a thousand pieces."** The CIA withheld information from the Warren Commission about Castro assassination plots. Key figures: **George de Mohrenschildt** (Oswald's handler with CIA connections); **David Atlee Phillips** (identified by anti-Castro leader Antonio Veciana as his CIA handler "Maurice Bishop" who met with Oswald); **E. Howard Hunt** (who made an alleged deathbed confession to his son, published in *Rolling Stone* in 2007, implicating CIA officers and LBJ). Jim Garrison prosecuted businessman **Clay Shaw** in 1969 — Shaw was acquitted, but CIA documents later confirmed he was indeed a CIA domestic contact.

### Mafia / organized crime

The HSCA found that **Carlos Marcello**, **Santos Trafficante Jr.**, and **Jimmy Hoffa** all had **"the motive, means, and opportunity."** Marcello, deported by RFK, reportedly said: **"If you cut off a dog's tail, the dog will only keep biting. But if you cut off its head, the dog will die."** Trafficante allegedly told Cuban exile Jose Aleman: **"Kennedy is going to be hit."** The strategic logic: killing JFK would remove RFK as Attorney General (LBJ loathed Bobby and would never keep him), ending the unprecedented crackdown on organized crime. This is exactly what happened. Jack Ruby's mob connections serve as the critical link — the HSCA concluded his shooting of Oswald **"was not a spontaneous act."**

### Cuban connections

**Anti-Castro Cubans** felt betrayed by JFK's withdrawal of air support at Bay of Pigs. The **Sylvia Odio incident** is among the most compelling evidence: two Cuban men and an American introduced as **"Leon Oswald"** visited her Dallas apartment in late September 1963. The next day, one caller said Oswald had commented that **"Kennedy should have been assassinated after the Bay of Pigs."** The HSCA found Odio's testimony **"essentially credible."**

Oswald's **Mexico City trip** remains deeply troubling: CIA surveillance captured someone impersonating him, the "mystery man" photo wasn't Oswald, and the agency failed to alert the FBI or Secret Service about Oswald's contact with KGB assassination specialist Kostikov in the six weeks before Dallas.

### LBJ theory

Proponents cite LBJ's political desperation (Bobby Baker scandal, possible removal from the 1964 ticket) and the claims of alleged mistress **Madeleine Duncan Brown**, who said LBJ whispered on the night of November 21: **"After tomorrow those goddamn Kennedys will never embarrass me again."** However, researcher David Perry demonstrated LBJ was documented at the Hotel Texas in Fort Worth that evening, not at the alleged Murchison party in Dallas. The theory is further weakened by the fact that **LBJ was in the motorcade** two cars behind JFK.

### Military-industrial complex

JFK signed **NSAM 263** on October 11, 1963, ordering withdrawal of 1,000 personnel from Vietnam. Four days after the assassination, LBJ signed **NSAM 273**, which conspiracy theorists argue reversed the withdrawal policy and led to massive escalation. The **Operation Northwoods** document (1962) — in which the Joint Chiefs unanimously proposed staging false-flag terrorist attacks on American cities to justify invading Cuba — demonstrated the military establishment's willingness to contemplate extreme measures. JFK rejected Northwoods and denied the Joint Chiefs chairman a second term.

### Secret Service failures

Nine of 28 agents went drinking after midnight on November 21–22; at least one stayed out until **5 AM**. The Cellar nightclub manager admitted agents were drinking **"pure Everclear"** (190-proof grain alcohol). The bubble top was removed. Driver Greer slowed instead of accelerating. Buildings along the route were not secured. No snipers were posted. Whether these represented incompetence or complicity remains debated.

### The "Three Tramps"

Three men arrested in a railroad car near Dealey Plaza were initially unidentified, leading to theories they were CIA operatives. In **1992**, journalist Mary La Fontaine identified them as **Harold Doyle, John Forrester Gedney, and Gus Abrams** — ordinary vagrants who had spent the previous night at a rescue mission. This theory is considered definitively debunked.

## 10. Suspicious deaths

The pattern of deaths among assassination-connected individuals has fueled conspiracy theories for decades. Among the most notable:

**Dorothy Kilgallen** (journalist who interviewed Ruby), died November 8, 1965, of a barbiturate overdose. She had told friends: **"If the wrong people knew what I know about the JFK assassination, it would cost me my life."** Her research files vanished. **George de Mohrenschildt** (Oswald's friend), died March 29, 1977, by shotgun blast ruled suicide — on the same day an HSCA investigator arrived. **David Ferrie** (CIA/Marcello associate), died February 22, 1967, just days after being named in Garrison's investigation. **Eladio del Valle** (anti-Castro Cuban associate of Ferrie) was murdered the **exact same day** — shot in the heart and struck with an axe.

**Sam Giancana** was murdered on June 19, 1975 — shot in the back of the head with a .22 — the night before he was to testify before the Church Committee. **Johnny Roselli** was murdered in August 1976 — asphyxiated, dismembered, and stuffed in a steel drum — after testifying to the Senate about CIA assassination plots. **Rose Cheramie** had told Louisiana police **two days before** the assassination that men she was traveling with were plotting to kill Kennedy in Dallas; she was killed by a car on September 4, 1965.

**Lee Bowers** (railroad tower witness who saw the "flash of light") died in a single-car accident on August 9, 1966. **Roger Craig** (deputy who claimed to see Oswald flee) died by gunshot in 1975 after years of harassment. **William Sullivan** (third-highest FBI official) was shot in a "hunting accident" on November 9, 1977 — before scheduled HSCA testimony. Seven FBI officials died within a six-month period before they were to testify.

Researcher Richard Charnin's statistical analysis found that of ~1,400 JFK-related witnesses, at least **78 died unnaturally** from 1964–1978 — when only ~18 would be statistically expected. Gerald Posner and Vincent Bugliosi counter that this reflects **selection bias**: any pool of 10,000+ tenuously connected people would include unusual deaths over decades.

## 11. Political and cultural context

### JFK vs. the national security establishment

The assassination occurred against a backdrop of extraordinary tension between Kennedy and the military-intelligence apparatus. The **Bay of Pigs fiasco** (April 1961) led JFK to fire the CIA's top leadership and vow to **"splinter the CIA into a thousand pieces."** The **Cuban Missile Crisis** (October 1962) saw JFK reject the Joint Chiefs' unanimous recommendation for air strikes and invasion, choosing a naval blockade and making a secret deal to remove Jupiter missiles from Turkey. His **American University "peace speech"** (June 10, 1963) called for nuclear disarmament: **"We all inhabit this small planet. We all breathe the same air. We all cherish our children's futures. And we are all mortal."** The **Nuclear Test Ban Treaty** followed within weeks.

### RFK's war on organized crime

As Attorney General, Robert Kennedy launched the most aggressive anti-Mafia campaign in history: organized crime convictions rose **800%** during his tenure. He targeted Carlos Marcello, Sam Giancana, Santos Trafficante, and especially Jimmy Hoffa. The mob had helped JFK win the 1960 election — Giancana's influence in Illinois was reportedly solicited by the Kennedy family — making the subsequent crackdown feel like a profound betrayal. The HSCA concluded there was **"solid evidence"** that Hoffa, Marcello, and Trafficante **"had discussions with their subordinates about murdering President Kennedy."**

The strategic calculation was elegant: killing RFK would only intensify JFK's prosecution of the mob, but killing JFK would remove RFK because LBJ (who loathed Bobby) would never keep him as Attorney General. On the afternoon of November 22, RFK confided to aide Ed Guthman: **"There's so much bitterness... I thought it would be me."**

### Dallas as "the City of Hate"

Dallas in 1963 was a hotbed of right-wing extremism. **General Edwin Walker** — forced from the Army by JFK for distributing John Birch Society literature — had made Dallas his base. UN Ambassador **Adlai Stevenson** was attacked by right-wing demonstrators in Dallas on October 24, 1963, hit with signs and spat upon. He warned Kennedy: **"There was something ugly and frightening about the atmosphere."** On the morning of November 22, **"Wanted for Treason" handbills** with JFK's mugshot-style photo circulated through Dallas, and the *Dallas Morning News* carried a hostile full-page ad bordered in black like a funeral notice.

### The CIA-Mafia plots against Castro

The Church Committee (1975) confirmed at least **eight CIA plots** to kill Castro from 1960–1965, including poison pills given to Mafia contacts (Giancana, Trafficante, Roselli), toxic cigars, exploding seashells, and contaminated diving suits. These CIA-Mafia assassination plots created a web of relationships between intelligence operatives, organized crime figures, and anti-Castro Cuban exiles that conspiracy researchers believe provided both the capability and motive for Kennedy's assassination.

## 12. The Assassination Records Review Board and ongoing declassification

The **JFK Records Act of 1992** mandated full disclosure of all assassination records by October 26, 2017. The **Assassination Records Review Board** (1994–1998) released approximately **5 million pages** of previously classified documents. Over **99%** of approximately 320,000 documents have been released.

On **January 23, 2025**, President Trump signed Executive Order 14176 directing full release of remaining classified records without redactions. On **March 18, 2025**, approximately **80,000 pages** were published — the largest single release. The FBI also discovered approximately **2,400 "newly inventoried" records** in 2025. Some records remain restricted under court-seal or tax-return protections. The JFK Assassination Records Collection at the National Archives now consists of **over 6 million pages** of records, photographs, films, and artifacts.

## Conclusion: what the evidence reveals and what remains unknown

The accumulated evidence points most strongly toward Lee Harvey Oswald as the shooter in Dealey Plaza. The physical evidence — the rifle, the bullets, the cartridge cases, his presence on the sixth floor, his flight, the Tippit murder — forms a coherent chain. The single-bullet theory, once derided, has been validated by 3D computer modeling and modern ballistic testing.

Yet the question of **whether Oswald acted alone** remains genuinely open. The CIA's withholding of information, Oswald's bewildering intelligence connections (Atsugi, the defection, de Mohrenschildt, 544 Camp Street, Mexico City), Ruby's documented mob ties, the HSCA's finding of "probable conspiracy," and the systematic destruction of evidence (burned autopsy notes, missing brain, unrecorded interrogations) create an evidentiary landscape that has resisted closure for over six decades.

For game design purposes, this case offers an almost inexhaustible web of real characters, contested evidence, parallel timelines, and unresolved mysteries. Every witness, every location, every piece of evidence is a node in a network of connections that players can trace — from the sniper's nest to the grassy knoll, from Minsk to Mexico City, from the Carousel Club to the sixth floor. The documented facts alone are stranger and more complex than any fiction.
